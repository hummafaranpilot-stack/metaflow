7. Pre-Launch Compliance Checklist

Google Ads has strict healthcare and supplement policies. Review every item below before launching any campaign. Non-compliance can result in ad disapproval, account suspension, or permanent bans.

Critical: Google's Healthcare and Medicines policy prohibits ads that make unsubstantiated health claims. Supplements cannot claim to "cure," "treat," "prevent," or "diagnose" any disease or medical condition. All copy in this document has been written to comply with these guidelines, but always verify against the latest Google Ads policies before launch.
  • No disease claims. Never use words like "cure," "treat," "prevent," "diagnose," "BPH," "benign prostatic hyperplasia," or reference any specific medical condition by name. Use "support," "promote," "maintain," and "healthy function" instead.
  • No before/after images. Google prohibits before-and-after imagery for health products. Use lifestyle photography of active, healthy men.
  • No misleading guarantees. You can mention the 60-day money-back guarantee, but do not imply guaranteed health outcomes (e.g., "guaranteed to work" or "100% effective").
  • Landing page must match ad claims. Ensure your landing page does not contain claims that go beyond what the ad states. Google reviews landing pages during ad approval.
  • Landing page must have required disclosures. Include a visible FDA disclaimer ("These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA..."), privacy policy link, terms of service link, and contact information.
  • No restricted pharmaceutical terms. Avoid terms like "prescription strength," "clinical strength," "pharmaceutical grade," or any comparison to prescription medications.
  • Use compliant testimonials. If using testimonials on your landing page, include a disclaimer that results are not typical and individual results may vary.
  • Set up conversion tracking properly. Use Google Ads conversion tracking or Google Analytics 4 goals. Without proper tracking, Smart Bidding strategies will underperform.
  • Verify your advertiser identity. Google requires advertiser verification for health-related ads in most markets. Complete this before launching.
  • Check geographic targeting restrictions. Some countries have stricter supplement advertising laws. Start with USA targeting and expand carefully.
  • Review ad extensions. Add sitelink extensions (e.g., "Ingredients," "Reviews," "Money-Back Guarantee," "How It Works") and callout extensions (e.g., "Free Shipping," "No Auto-Ship," "Made in USA").
  • Test your landing page load speed. Google factors page speed into Quality Score. Use PageSpeed Insights and aim for a score above 70 on mobile.

8. Final Notes for Media Buyers

Optimization strategies and practical advice for scaling PowerX Pro campaigns on Google Ads.

Campaign Structure Recommendations

Strategy Notes
Start with Search, then expand to PMax. Search campaigns give you the clearest intent signal and the most control over keyword targeting. Once you have 30+ conversions, layer in a Performance Max campaign to capture additional demand across YouTube, Display, Gmail, and Discover. Recommended campaign structure: - Campaign 1: Search -- Prostate Health Keywords (exact + phrase match) - Campaign 2: Search -- Male Vitality Keywords (exact + phrase match) - Campaign 3: Performance Max -- Full asset group with all text, image, and video assets - Campaign 4 (optional): YouTube -- Video action campaigns using the 15s and 30s scripts Bidding strategy progression: 1. Start with Manual CPC or Maximize Clicks (with a bid cap) for the first 2-4 weeks 2. Switch to Target CPA once you have 15-30 conversions in a 30-day window 3. Test Target ROAS only if you are tracking revenue values accurately Budget allocation: - Allocate 60-70% of budget to Search campaigns initially - Allocate 20-30% to PMax once Search is profitable - Reserve 10% for YouTube testing if you have video assets

Audience Targeting Tips

Audience Notes
Demographics: - Age: 45-65+ (primary), 35-44 (secondary for early-stage prostate awareness) - Gender: Male only - Household income: Top 50% (supplement buyers tend to skew higher income) - Parental status: Not a strong signal for this product; leave open In-Market Audiences (for observation or targeting): - Health & Fitness > Vitamins & Supplements - Health & Fitness > Health Products - Combine with age/gender layering for best results Custom Intent Audiences (for PMax and Display): - Build custom audiences based on competitor URLs and prostate-related search terms - Include URLs of top prostate supplement brands and health information sites - Include search terms: "best prostate supplement," "prostate health," "Saw Palmetto benefits," "frequent urination at night men" Remarketing: - Set up a remarketing list for landing page visitors who did not convert - Create a 7-day, 14-day, and 30-day remarketing audience - Use different messaging for each window (urgency increases with recency)

Ad Testing and Iteration

Testing Notes
RSA testing approach: - Run 2-3 RSAs per ad group maximum - Pin your strongest headline in position 1 and strongest CTA headline in position 2 - Let Google rotate the remaining headlines and descriptions - Review "Asset Details" in the Ads tab after 2,000+ impressions to see performance ratings Key metrics to monitor: - CTR by ad: Aim for 4%+ on Search, 0.5%+ on Display - Conversion rate: 2-5% is healthy for supplement landing pages - Cost per acquisition (CPA): Calculate your max CPA based on your affiliate commission - Quality Score: Check keyword-level QS weekly; aim for 7+ across core terms - Search impression share: Monitor to ensure you are not losing impression share to budget Negative keyword management: - Add negatives aggressively in the first 2 weeks using the Search Terms report - Common negatives for this niche: "free," "reddit," "side effects," "scam," "review," "FDA," "cancer," "symptoms," "surgery," "medication," "prescription," "wiki" - Create a shared negative keyword list and apply it across all campaigns Landing page split testing: - Test long-form VSL pages against shorter direct-response pages - Test different hero images (product shot vs. lifestyle vs. ingredients) - Test price display (showing vs. hiding price above the fold) - Use Google Ads Experiments or a third-party tool for statistical rigor

Scaling Playbook

Scaling Notes
Phase 1 -- Validate (Week 1-3): - Daily budget: $50-100/day across all campaigns - Goal: Find 2-3 profitable keywords and 1 winning ad combination - Action: Pause underperformers aggressively. Focus budget on winners. Phase 2 -- Optimize (Week 3-6): - Daily budget: $100-250/day - Goal: Achieve consistent CPA below your breakeven threshold - Action: Switch to automated bidding. Add PMax campaign. Expand keyword lists. Phase 3 -- Scale (Week 6+): - Daily budget: $250-1,000+/day - Goal: Maintain CPA while increasing volume - Action: Increase budgets by 15-20% every 3-5 days (avoid shocking the algorithm). Add YouTube video campaigns. Test new audience segments. Expand to CA/UK/AU markets. Common pitfalls to avoid: - Do not increase budget by more than 20% in a single day - Do not change bidding strategy and budget simultaneously - Do not judge performance on fewer than 50 clicks per ad variation - Do not ignore mobile vs. desktop performance splits - Do not run without a negative keyword list from day one
Final Reminder: All copy in this document is provided as a starting point. The best-performing ads are the ones you test, iterate, and refine based on real data from your specific audience and landing page. Use these swipes to get launched quickly, then optimize relentlessly.
