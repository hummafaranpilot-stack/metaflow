Quality Score Optimization

Quality Score directly impacts your CPC and ad rank. A Quality Score of 7+ means you pay less per click than competitors. Here's how to maximize it for supplement ads.

The Three Quality Score Factors

Factor Weight How to Optimize for Supplement Ads
Expected CTR ~39%
  • Use exact and phrase match keywords to ensure tight relevance between search query and ad
  • Include the primary keyword in Headline 1 and Headline 2 (pin if needed)
  • Test emotional triggers in descriptions: urgency, fear of missing out, social proof numbers
  • Use ad extensions to increase visible ad size and improve CTR
Ad Relevance ~22%
  • Create tightly themed ad groups with 5-15 closely related keywords each
  • Write ad copy that directly mirrors the keywords in each ad group
  • Create separate ad groups for: prostate supplement, frequent urination, nighttime urination, saw palmetto, etc.
  • Do NOT put all keywords in one ad group — this destroys ad relevance
Landing Page Experience ~39%
  • Ensure your landing page loads in under 3 seconds (use Google PageSpeed Insights)
  • The landing page headline must echo the ad's primary message (message match)
  • Include the target keyword naturally on the landing page, especially in the H1 and first paragraph
  • Mobile responsiveness is mandatory — over 60% of health searches happen on mobile
  • Include trust signals: money-back guarantee badge, secure checkout icons, customer count
  • Have visible contact information, privacy policy, and terms of service

Compliance Checklist

Google Ads has specific policies for healthcare and supplement advertising. Violating these can result in ad disapproval, account suspension, or permanent bans. Microsoft Ads follows similar but slightly more lenient policies.

Google Ads Healthcare & Supplements Policy

  • Supplements are permitted in Google Ads in most countries, including the US, UK, CA, and AU.
  • No disease claims: You cannot claim that PowerX Pro treats, cures, or prevents BPH, cancer, or any disease.
  • No misleading claims: Avoid superlatives like "the only," "guaranteed cure," or "100% effective."
  • Use structure/function claims only: "Supports prostate health," "promotes healthy urinary flow," "helps maintain normal prostate function."
  • FDA disclaimer required on landing page: "These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
  • Landing page must have: Contact information, privacy policy, terms of service, and clear refund policy.
  • No before/after imagery: Google prohibits before-and-after comparisons for supplement ads.
  • No fake urgency: Countdown timers that reset on refresh, fake "limited stock" warnings, or fabricated scarcity are policy violations.

Landing Page Compliance Audit

Before submitting your ads, verify your landing page passes every item on this checklist.

  • FDA disclaimer visible on the page (footer minimum; near claims preferred)
  • Privacy policy linked and accessible
  • Terms and conditions linked and accessible
  • Refund/return policy clearly stated
  • Physical address or customer service contact information present
  • No auto-playing video with sound
  • No exit-intent popups (Google may flag these as deceptive)
  • No fake testimonials or fabricated doctor endorsements
  • "Results may vary" or "Individual results not guaranteed" near testimonials
  • No countdown timers that reset on page refresh
  • HTTPS/SSL certificate active (no mixed content warnings)
  • Page loads in under 3 seconds on mobile
  • Mobile-responsive layout (no horizontal scrolling, readable text without zooming)
  • Affiliate disclosure present if you earn commissions

Common Disapproval Reasons & Fixes

Disapproval Reason What It Means How to Fix
"Healthcare and medicines" Your ad or landing page contains language that implies treating a medical condition Replace disease claims with structure/function claims. Remove words like "treat," "cure," "remedy," "disease."
"Misleading content" Claims on your landing page are considered exaggerated or unsubstantiated Soften claims with qualifiers ("may," "supports," "helps promote"). Remove fake urgency elements.
"Destination not working" Your landing page is slow, returns errors, or has broken elements Fix page speed, resolve 404 errors, ensure SSL is active. Test on multiple devices.
"Restricted content" Your ad targets a restricted category (healthcare/supplements) without proper account setup Ensure your Google Ads account has passed healthcare advertiser verification (if required in your country).
"Unacceptable business practices" Your landing page uses deceptive tactics (fake reviews, misleading checkout flows, hidden charges) Remove fake elements. Ensure checkout is transparent. Display pricing clearly before purchase.
Account Safety: If you receive a policy violation, fix the issue and request a review through Google Ads. Do NOT create a new ad account to circumvent violations — Google will detect this and permanently ban all associated accounts. Treat every policy warning seriously.

Microsoft Ads Differences

Microsoft Ads (Bing): Microsoft Ads generally follows similar policies to Google but tends to be slightly more lenient in enforcement. However, do not assume something that gets disapproved on Google will pass on Microsoft. Key differences:
  • Microsoft may allow slightly more aggressive claims in descriptions, but still prohibits disease cure claims.
  • Microsoft's review process is often faster (same day vs. 1-3 days for Google).
  • Microsoft requires the same landing page elements: privacy policy, contact info, FDA disclaimer.
  • If your Google campaigns are approved, you can typically import them to Microsoft Ads with minimal policy issues.
