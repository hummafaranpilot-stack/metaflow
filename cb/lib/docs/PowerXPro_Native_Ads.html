Images to Avoid

  • Product bottle images in thumbnails (too "salesy," kills CTR on native)
  • Overly polished stock photos of smiling models (feels fake, reduces trust)
  • Anatomical diagrams or medical illustrations (may violate network policies)
  • Before/after comparisons (policy violation on most native platforms)
  • Images implying sexual performance (will get flagged and rejected)
  • Red arrows, circles, or clickbait visual gimmicks (reduces approval rates)
Thumbnail Best Practice: Test at least 5 different thumbnails per headline. On Taboola and Outbrain, the thumbnail drives more variance in CTR than the headline itself. Always A/B test candid vs. medical authority images against each headline.

Compliance Notes

Native ad networks have strict policies for health supplements. Violating these policies will result in ad rejection, account warnings, or permanent bans. Follow these guidelines carefully.

Universal Rules (All Networks)

  • Always label advertorial pages as "Advertisement," "Sponsored Content," or "Paid Content"
  • Never claim PowerX Pro cures, treats, or prevents any disease (including BPH, cancer, or prostatitis)
  • Use "supports," "promotes," "may help," or "is associated with" instead of definitive claims
  • Include the standard FDA disclaimer: "These statements have not been evaluated by the FDA. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."
  • Never use fake news site branding (e.g., CNN, Fox News, WebMD logos or styling)
  • All testimonials must include "Results may vary" or "Individual results not guaranteed"
  • Never use images or names of celebrities, public figures, or doctors without explicit permission
  • Advertorial pages must have a clear, accessible privacy policy and terms of service

Headline Compliance

Avoid These Patterns:
  • "Doctors hate this trick" — implies medical suppression, will be rejected
  • "Cure your prostate problems" — disease cure claims violate FTC and network policies
  • "Clinically proven to shrink your prostate" — overly specific medical claim
  • "Better than Flomax / Finasteride" — drug comparison claims will trigger review
  • "FDA approved natural remedy" — supplements are not FDA approved; this is misleading

Platform-Specific Tips

Each native ad network has different algorithms, approval processes, and optimization levers. Here's how to maximize performance on the big three.

Taboola

Campaign Setup
  • Campaign Objective: Use "Online Purchases" or "Lead Generation" — never "Brand Awareness" for supplement offers.
  • Bidding Strategy: Start with Smart Bid (Target CPA) at $40-60 CPA target. Switch to Manual CPC once you have 30+ conversions and understand your CPCs.
  • Budget: Minimum $50/day per campaign for the algorithm to optimize. Ideally $100-200/day during testing phase.
  • Creative Load: Submit 10-15 headline/thumbnail combinations per campaign. Taboola's algorithm needs volume to find winners.
Targeting Best Practices
  • OS Targeting: Separate campaigns for desktop and mobile. Desktop typically converts better for prostate supplements (older demographic).
  • Publisher Blocking: After 48 hours, block any publishers with 1,000+ impressions and zero conversions. Review weekly.
  • Geo: Start with US-only. Tier 1 English-speaking countries (CA, UK, AU) can be added after proving the funnel profitably in US.
  • Day Parting: Prostate supplement ads convert best between 6 AM - 10 AM and 8 PM - 12 AM (when men are experiencing symptoms).
Approval Tips
  • Avoid words like "cure," "guaranteed results," or "miracle" in headlines — these trigger automatic rejection.
  • Landing pages must load in under 3 seconds. Taboola will penalize slow-loading pages.
  • Advertorial pages need an "Advertisement" label clearly visible above the fold.
  • Submit your landing page for whitelist review with your account manager before scaling.
  • If rejected, resubmit with softened language. Don't create a new account — this can result in a network ban.

Outbrain

Campaign Setup
  • Campaign Objective: Select "Conversions" and install the Outbrain pixel on your thank-you page for accurate tracking.
  • Bidding: Use "Conversion Bid Strategy" (CBS) with a target CPA of $45-65. Start with "Semi-Automatic" bidding for the first 2 weeks.
  • Budget: $75-150/day minimum during learning phase. Outbrain's algorithm needs at least 20 conversions per week to optimize effectively.
  • Creative Testing: Submit at least 6 headlines and 8 thumbnails per ad variation. Outbrain tests combinations automatically.
Key Differences from Taboola
  • Stricter Content Policies: Outbrain is more conservative than Taboola. Avoid aggressive curiosity gaps and implied medical claims.
  • Premium Publisher Network: Outbrain has higher-quality publisher inventory (CNN, Bloomberg, etc.), which means higher CPCs but better traffic quality.
  • Section Targeting: Use Outbrain's section targeting to place ads on health-related content sections of publisher sites.
  • Lookalike Audiences: Once you have 1,000+ converters, create a Lookalike audience from your pixel data. This typically reduces CPA by 20-35%.
Approval Tips
  • Outbrain requires advertorial pages to look and feel like editorial content — not sales pages.
  • No countdown timers, fake scarcity, or "only X bottles left" messaging on the landing page.
  • All health claims must be supportable with published research. Keep references accessible.
  • Outbrain manually reviews health supplement ads. Expect 24-72 hour approval times. Be patient; do not resubmit the same ad multiple times.

MGID

Campaign Setup
  • Campaign Type: Choose "Performance" campaign type for supplement offers.
  • Bidding: Start with Manual CPC at $0.15-0.40 per click. MGID does not have as sophisticated auto-bidding as Taboola/Outbrain, so manual optimization is key.
  • Budget: $30-75/day is sufficient for testing on MGID. Lower traffic volumes but potentially cheaper CPCs.
  • GEO Focus: MGID has strong inventory in US, but also performs well in European and LATAM markets if you have localized funnels.
Optimization Strategy
  • Widget/Publisher Blocking: MGID requires aggressive publisher management. Check daily for the first week. Block any widget spending $5+ with no conversions.
  • Creative Rotation: MGID's algorithm favors the first creatives you upload. If performance drops, create a new campaign with fresh creatives rather than adding to existing.
  • Selective GEO: On MGID, narrow to specific US states with the highest conversion rates for health supplements: FL, TX, CA, AZ, PA, OH, NC.
  • Traffic Quality: Monitor bounce rates closely. MGID can have higher bot traffic than Taboola/Outbrain. Use server-side click validation if possible.
Approval Tips
  • MGID is generally more lenient than Outbrain but still requires compliance with health supplement advertising standards.
  • Landing pages must have clear navigation, no deceptive elements, and accessible legal pages.
  • Work directly with your MGID account manager for pre-approval of your funnel before scaling spend.
  • MGID offers "Selective Bidding" by widget — use this to increase bids on high-performing publishers and decrease on poor performers.
